Newton Raphson Method

Definition

The Newton–Raphson method is an iterative numerical technique used to find approximate roots of a real-valued equation f(x) = 0. It starts with an initial guess and improves the accuracy of the solution by repeatedly applying a mathematical formula.

Formula

xn+1 = xn − f(xn) f′(xn) ,   n ≥ 0

Examples (3 Iterations)

Example 1

Given:
f(x) = 4x − ex
f′(x) = 4 − ex
x₀ = 2.5

Example 2

Given:
f(x) = 2x³ − 3x − 6
f′(x) = 6x² − 3
x₀ = 1.5

Fun Fact

Newton–Raphson converges very quickly when the starting guess is close to the true root. Often the number of correct digits roughly doubles with each iteration.
